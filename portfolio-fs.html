  • Host: Imperial College London

Imperial Formula Racing has been an incredible experience that has allowed me to dive headfirst into the exciting world of motorsports. As the Head of Electronics, I have the privilege of leading a talented team of ten individuals within a 100+ member inter-departmental project. Our main objective is to deliver an impressive Electric Vehicle that will compete at the renowned Silverstone track in the Formula Student UK 2023 competition.

A significant aspect of my role involves overseeing the implementation of digital logic design and programmable safety systems. Working with C++ and Arduino Due hardware, we ensure that our designs align with the competition's specifications and prioritize fault tolerance. It's an intriguing challenge that keeps me fully engaged, knowing that our efforts will contribute to the success of our electric race car.

Furthermore, I actively collaborate with esteemed academic advisors in the field of PCB (Printed Circuit Board) design. Together, we aim to develop high and low voltage PCB designs that prioritize safety and functionality. By utilizing the Eagle software, we meticulously craft our designs, paying careful attention to every detail. It's a rewarding process that allows us to push the boundaries of innovation while ensuring the reliability of our vehicle.

Imperial Formula Racing has provided me with an exceptional platform to channel my passion for electronics. Being at the forefront of this groundbreaking project is truly gratifying. As we approach the Formula Student UK 2023 competition, I am confident in our team's abilities and committed to achieving excellence. I look forward to witnessing our hard work come to life on the track, as our Electric Vehicle captivates the audience and showcases the remarkable results of our dedicated efforts.
