During my time at Imperial College, I had the incredible opportunity to be part of the EEE Intelligent Digital Systems Lab, where I worked on the development of FpgaConvNet, a Python-based software suite. This suite was specifically designed to map convolutional neural networks to FPGAs, and I was thrilled to contribute to its advancement.

From August 2022 to October 2022, I focused on the development of the FpgaConvNet Demonstrator. My main responsibilities included fixing bugs, enhancing stability, and creating detailed documentation. Additionally, I collaborated closely with doctoral students from the University of Strathclyde to explore and showcase the suite's potential applications. One of the highlights was my work on developing a Mnist classifier using Keras, which involved training and evaluating the classifier on a ZYNQ 7 SoC-based demonstrator using Xilinx Vivado HLS and Vivado suites.

Another fascinating project I worked on was the development of a modulation classifier. Through extensive research, I identified its promising application in RF modulation classification under multi-path fading. I also had the opportunity to address usability issues that were not previously considered by the core development team, either by reporting them or resolving them myself.

Prior to that, from July 2021 to October 2021, I was involved in the development of the FpgaConvNet Optimiser. My main focus during this period was automating the Design Space Exploration process. I analyzed the performance of the optimizer and implemented new simulated annealing techniques, such as periodic restarting and automated annealer parameter estimation. These enhancements led to a remarkable 10x improvement in optimizer performance for certain workloads.

Working on FpgaConvNet at the EEE Intelligent Digital Systems Lab was an incredibly rewarding experience. I had the opportunity to contribute to cutting-edge research, collaborate with talented individuals, and push the boundaries of what is possible in the field of convolutional neural networks and FPGAs. This hands-on experience allowed me to deepen my knowledge and skills while making a meaningful impact in the development of this innovative software suite.
